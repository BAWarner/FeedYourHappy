The Feed Your Happy app:Master the skills of everyday happiness

We’ve gameified positive psychology Now the skills to happiness become actionable steps for anyone looking for more in their life. #feedyourhappy

Top-tier universities have discovered something amazing—happiness is more than just a mood or a feeling—it’s a skill.

Now we’ve systematized it so anyone can do it. We’ve made it actionable. Easy. Fun. Introducing Feed Your Happy the app that helps you practice the skills that lead to everyday happiness.

Feed Your Happy works on a digital card system. Positive psychology concepts are broken down into bite-sized activity cards under seven key skills:
  1. BE GRATEFUL – Gratitude is known as the universal happiness skill. You can’t be grateful and unhappy any more than you can be positive and negative at the same time. Make a choice. Intentionally choosing to be grateful is the easiest and first step to learning to focus your energy in positive directions.
  2. ENJOY THE NOW – Life is a journey. Not a destination. Enjoy the ride. Goals are good and important, but if we focus our energy too much on the future we’ll miss the available joy that comes in the moment. Don’t procrastinate your happiness. Don’t hope for it in the future. Expect it. Anticipate it. Make it.
  3. LIFT SOMEONE ELSE – Looking for an instant happiness boost? Practice committing random acts of kindness. Consciously. Deliberately. They don’t have to be earth-shatteringly huge, but they must be premeditated. You must choose to care. It’s all about triggering a positive energy exchange. Take charge.
  4. DON’T WORRY – As Mahatma Ghandi so eloquently stated, “Nothing wastes the body like worry.” Worry is powerful, but negative energy. It’s creative and imaginative. Yet also destructive. It brings fear, stress, and anxiety. Reverse the flow. Learn to rewrite your endings to envision a positive outcome.
  5. FOSTER RELATIONSHIPS – Connections matter. Hold onto the people around you. Humans are social creatures. We need each other. Studies show the strong social support can positively impact health, influence recovery, and generate top-level performance.
  6. IMPROVE DAILY – Goals are important. But it’s not the completion, it’s the direction that matters. Striving toward our potential brings self-confidence, personal pride, and peace. Not striving leads to stagnation. Aim high. You won’t get discouraged by the distance yet to travel if you learn to recognize and reward yourself for the milestones you’ve reached along the way.
  7. BEGIN AGAIN – We crave the new. Learn to renew. Reaching a plateau is a natural part of life, but can feel negative. Remember the energy you put into relationships when they were new—the courting, the caring, the nuturing? You can rejuvinate your old relationships by putting in that same energy. Nothing is ever final. Each day is a new opportunity to begin again.

Here’s how it works

Flip through the activity cards to discover concepts, exercises, tips, quotes, challenges and media that appeal to you. Pick the ones you like. Add them to your action plan. Flip the cards over to set yourself reminders. Daily. Weekly. Monthly. Or at multiple or random times of the day. Whatever suits you best.

Pick activities that bring you balance or improve your relationships. Eliminate worry or negative energy from your life. Set goals. Go after your dreams. Spread encouragement.
